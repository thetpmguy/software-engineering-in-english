# 📚 Resources 19 — The Free Canon & Career Reality Checks

All free (or free to audit). This list is short on purpose — the lesson warned you about
drowning. Pick **one** learning resource matched to your target role, plus the reality-
check sites as needed.

---

## 🎓 Learn (pick ONE to start — seriously, one)

- **[freeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org)** — a huge, free, project-based
  curriculum covering web development, Python, and data analysis, run by a beloved
  nonprofit. You earn as you build, and its forum is one of the kindest beginner
  communities on the internet. **Best for:** self-taught learners who want structure
  with flexibility; the data-analysis track is the Excel→SQL bridge from Module 12 made
  walkable.

- **[The Odin Project](https://www.theodinproject.com)** — a free, full web-development
  course famous for *not* holding your hand: it sends you to real documentation and
  makes you set up real tools, exactly like a working developer. Harder than
  freeCodeCamp, and its graduates say that's precisely why it works. **Best for:**
  aspiring frontend/full-stack developers who liked figuring things out in this course's
  labs.

- **[CS50](https://cs50.harvard.edu/x)** — Harvard's legendary introduction to computer
  science, free online, taught with theatrical joy to a lecture hall. Deeper and more
  theoretical than the other two; the famous first weeks are genuinely challenging.
  **Best for:** readers who loved Parts 1 and 4 of this course and want the
  university-grade version — after this course, you're ready for it.

## 🗺️ Navigate

- **[roadmap.sh](https://roadmap.sh)** — famous community-built diagrams showing every
  skill on the path to each role (frontend, backend, data analyst, QA, product…).
  **A warning with the recommendation:** these maps can overwhelm — they show *everything
  a senior knows*, not what a beginner needs Monday. Use one as a map to check where you
  are, never as a to-do list to complete. **Best for:** the "what should I learn next?"
  moments in your 6-month plan.

- **[levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi)** — real, self-reported tech salaries by role,
  company, and level. Two honest uses: seeing what roles genuinely pay in your region
  (the numbers skew toward big companies and big cities — adjust expectations
  accordingly), and knowing the going rate before you answer an offer. **Best for:** the
  negotiation stage — and for replacing salary myths with data.

## 🎓 A note on Coursera and edX

Both platforms host university courses (including well-known ones on Python, data, and
design) that can be **audited for free** — you watch all the lectures and do the
readings, paying only if you want the certificate. Since this module taught you that a
portfolio beats certificates, the free audit path is usually all you need. Look for the
small "Audit" or "audit this course" link during enrollment; it's sometimes tucked below
the shiny paid button.

## 🔍 A note on searching for more

Career-advice searches attract the two lies from the lesson — "you can't without a
degree" and "hired in 90 days!" — plus an ocean of influencers selling shortcuts. Your
filters: anyone promising speed is selling something; anyone gatekeeping is projecting;
and the boring, free, famous resources above have outlasted a thousand shiny paid ones
for a reason. When in doubt, ask your community (Lab 19, Part 5) — real people's recent
experience beats any listicle.
